About

Expensive Photography drones are a thing of the past. Why use a DJI droneâ€™s camera when you got an iPhone XS? At StratoSnap we want to make aerial photography much more accessible for the average smartphone user. StratoSnap is a fully Autonomous Drone capable of utilizing your smartphone camera to capture memories on your next adventure.

Portable

StratoSnap's compact design allows you to bring it on your adventures. It can be easily be disassembled and stored in traveling gear then assembled again when at a picturesque spot.

Intelligent

StratoSnap can reach heights optimal for aeriel photography with its automated take off, flight, and landing modes. It utilizes machine learning and image processing to capture the clearest shots

Easy To Use

Simply attach your phone using the gimbal and tune your photo preferences. Leave the camera balancing and stabalization to StratoSnap. No need to port over picutres from your SD card, your pictures are saved on your phone

Technology

Unlike expensive photography drones which include a high quality camera, sensors, and other components, StratoSnap harnesses the fantastic hardware already found on modern Smartphones to make these drones much more accessible. StratoSnap provides an easy-to-use drone smarphone case capable of carrying and stabilizing your smartphone for aerial photography. The attachment communicates with your smartphone via bluetooth using the StratoSnap iOS application. The user would simply input the desired settings in the mobile application, snap it on to the drone and hold it for take off. StratoSnap will then fly to the desired position and take the pictures using facial detection and other various computer vision techniques to capture the optimal image. Once the pictures are taken the drone will return to its original position for retreival.

Development of first prototype

A large testing frame was built to test concepts. The large frame provided much needed space for prototyping autopilot features and other hardware changes. It provided ample thrust and stability even with payload.

  • Date: January 14, 2019

Flight controller tuning

Continuous improvement and tuning was performed for our testing drone in manual flying mode. Flight modes and other characteristics were learned. This phase involved crashes and the team was able to observe points of failure, and areas of challenge.

  • Date: January 21, 2019

Auto-pilot development

Development of the autopilot module was initiated. The architechture involved a raspberry pi zero feeding in input to the flight controller via PWM. This was developed to emulate the traditional remote transmitter actions. The software system needed to be deployed on the raspberry pi zero to support remote arming and flight, controlled by the smartphone app. Autonomous take off and landing was tested this week using the large drone.

  • Date: January 28, 2019

StratoSnap App Development

The mobile App acts as the brain of the drone, directing its flight and gimbal position to take the optimal photos. The main camera functionality of the was built and facial detection algorithms using CoreML were implemented. Meanwhile on the mechanical side, initial gimbal designs were modelled.

  • Date: February 4, 2019

Gimbal Integration

After a few iterations of the gimbal design, one was chosen to be 3d printed. Design priority was given to maintaining a centre of mass directly at the centre of the drone frame. This ensures the best stability during flight. Damping washers where used between the connection of the gimbal and drone frame to reduce vibrations in the picture.

  • Date: February 11, 2019

Development of smaller drone

Once all prototyping was done on the larger frame, the concepts were applied to develop a smaller drone. This one contained higher power output motors due to it housing smaller propellers. The same flight controller and raspberry pi was reused from the larger prototype.

  • Date: February 18, 2019

PID Tuning Small Frame

Majority of this week was spent tuning the control loops of the flight controller to ensure good stability of the drone. Weight distribution was modified and the heavy payload acted as a challenge for stability. Crashes, once again occurred at this stage and repairs were made throughout. Different landing gears were tried, ranging from PVC to wooden dowels.

  • Date: February 25, 2019

Testing with the fully integrated drone

Final components were chosen and fixed to the drone. Wooden landing gear capable of screwing on to the frame was integrated. This provided the best protection during crashes and a screw joint made it much easier to switch out. Fine tuning of the drone was also performed with the autonomous mode using the GPS. Manual mode was integrated in for safety during autonomous flights.

  • Date: March 4, 2019

Symposium Week

This is an exciting week! The symposium is at the end of the week, and the team is scrambling together to complete final tweaking to our drone.
